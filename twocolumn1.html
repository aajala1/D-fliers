The D-Fliers project explores the intersection of artificial intelligence and climate data to predict flight delays. By analyzing real-time and historical weather patterns alongside flight data, our model enhances airspace resilience and supports better decision-making for airlines and passengers alike.

  • Develop a machine learning model for accurate flight delay prediction
  • Incorporate real-time climate data to improve predictive power
  • Utilize explainable AI to understand feature impact and ensure transparency
  • Improve operational planning and passenger communication for airlines

Our research uses FAA historical flight data and real-time NOAA weather data. We preprocess, clean, and engineer features to train an XGBoost regression model. Model performance is evaluated using MAE, RMSE, and RÂ² metrics. Explainable AI tools like SHAP and LIME are integrated to interpret the influence of key variables.

  • Tools: Python, Pandas, Scikit-learn, XGBoost, SHAP, LIME
  • Techniques: Feature engineering, cross-validation, model interpretation

Initial results show promising predictive accuracy, with weather-related features like wind speed, temperature variance, and precipitation ranking highest in influence. SHAP visualizations reveal nuanced insights into how these features interact with delays, guiding further optimization.
